Not giving up on programming. Thanks to NeoG

27 Sep 2021 | Yogesh Gupta

I randomly came across a tweet of Tanay announcing something about NeoG. I just thought this is just another programming bootcamp like so many others. I had applied to some of them earlier but they did not accept me because of my background. But more i read and dug deeper about NeoG I instantly felt I should give this a shot.

I tried to learn to program many times in the past. More precisely 6 times in the last 5 years and I failed. I followed different youtube courses, udacity, codecademy etc among so many others. Each time i would pick up new programming language or a learning platform thinking this is easier and more faster as per the google search and youtube videos. I failed everytime. I realised now why i was failing all these past years while i started learning at NeoG.

Reasons I failed in the past
1. Watching videos or learning? - I started learning by watching youtube videos. While it is a great platform for learning new things. But in my case it did not work perfectly for learning programming. Like majority of people I used to simply watch the video and do nothing. Watching the videos gave a sense of accomplishment but I was not learning anything concrete just becasuse of no implementation.
2. No Community - I was not a part of the community of learners or developers. This made learning bland. Being a part of the community helps a lot. I cant imagine learning so much at NeoG till now without the community
3. No job assistance/guidance - The biggest reason of my failure. This lead me to give up in just few weeks after starting to learn. I am learning on codecademy but what after that ? Who would hire me ? I have a non-tech background. There was absolutely no assitance and support leading to job after learning. I thought I am shooting in the dark.
4. Background - My background was a big hinderance while applying to bootcamps online. They would simply take the recent engineering passouts with high fees and Income sharing agreements.They did not accept people with non-tech background
